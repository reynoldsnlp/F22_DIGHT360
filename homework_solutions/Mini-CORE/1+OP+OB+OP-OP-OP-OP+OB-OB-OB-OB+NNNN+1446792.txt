<h> Public Relations 101: A list of who should be seen at Brooklyn Nets games
<p> So tonight was supposed to be the home opener for the Brooklyn Nets before it got postponed due to hurricane difficulties. But regardless I thought I would take the opportunity for a basic public relations exercise. There's already plenty of other commentary about what the new Barclays Center in Brooklyn means  for Brooklyn, the NBA, the NHL, Long Island, urban renewal, hipsters, real estate values and plenty else (see Grantland or the WSJ for some basic primers). And all of that is interesting and worthy and crucial to know for your basic New York scenester. But of greater interest to me is more basic: if the Nets wind up being losers, how can investors ensure this remains a viable enterprise? Herewith, a short list of people (besides Jay-Z) best positioned for the mutual publicity reaping from sitting in the front row of a Brooklyn Nets home game:
